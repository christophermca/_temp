Description:

This souvenir spoon is from Blois, France, a town popular for its beautiful Renaissance chateau, Château de Blois. The finial of the spoon shows the emblem of Queen Anne of Brittany (Anne de Bretagne), the collared ermine beneath the crown. The ermine represents purity and honor, her motto: Mulo mori quam faedari, “Better to die than be sullied". Queen Anne passed in the Château de Blois in the early 1500s. Below the ermine is a coffe bean, the stem of the spoon is made to look like a bear branch and the back of the bowl is sculpted to look like a leaf. The bowl of the spoon is gold and on the interior of the bowl is written Blois, in delicate script.
